4. Furniture for Offices

Past practice for the purchase of furniture for faculty offices has been for these items to be paid by the areas or individual faculty except for state issued furniture by the university. The college will continue to follow this practice.

5. Classroom Update

  • The approval for the remodeling of rooms in the building is continuing to progress. Terri
    will be meeting with a campus committee this week to finalize the plans. Tentative plans
    are:
    • Room 201 will be split off with the center of the room remaining the same with a seating capacity of 42. A wall will be inserted at each end of the room creating two breakout rooms with doors from the hall into each breakout room.
    • Room 204 will be split in half creating two rooms with seating capacity of 35 and 34-35 and a possible reorientation of 90 degrees.
    • Room 205 will have some of the fixed seats removed and replaced with movable furniture increasing the seating capacity to around 40.
  • The Accounting Area will be remodeling 206 this summer.
  • The college has requested three rooms in another building but no decision has been
    made. College has offered to improve these rooms in exchange for first right of
    scheduling.
  • Summer quarter classes scheduled in the rooms to be remodeled will be reassigned to
    other rooms. The goal is for the remodeled rooms to be ready for use Fall Quarter.

6. Summer Quarter Schedule

  • Areas were asked to review the number of courses offered this summer and compare
    the number with last summer course offerings. Core courses that are in high demand
    should be scheduled first.
  • The new senior project guidelines will be in effect with Summer Quarter.
  • Senior projects will be under the supervision of certain faculty for Summer Quarter.
