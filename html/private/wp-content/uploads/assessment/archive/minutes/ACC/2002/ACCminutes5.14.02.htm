Area Chairs Council
Meeting Notes - May 14, 2002

Present: Fred Abitia, Norm Borin, Doug Cerf, Barry Floyd, Tad Miller, Ken Riener, Jim Sena, Alden Shiers, Terri Swartz

1. Approval of Notes:

  • Meeting notes of the May 7, 2002, meeting were approved by email and posted on OCOB.

2. Course Evaluation Proposal

  • Two areas reported back that the current guidelines were acceptable and no change was
    necessary. Two areas expressed concern about creating a cumbersome process.
  • It was decided that directions for administering the evaluations would be standardized in the areas/college. Jim Sena and Alden Shiers will work with Sharon Leib and DimitriXavier to develop these guidelines. At this time no further changes in process would
    occur.

3. Senior Project and Independent Study Course Proposal

  • The content of the current proposal will be revised by Ken Riener and Norm Borin and will be submitted again at the next ACC meeting.

4. Zero Teaching Proposal

  • One area expressed concern about the appropriate timing of this proposal given the strategic planning initiative. Other areas felt the proposal had potential but additional details needed to be added.
  • The intent of this proposal was to provide more support for the untenured tenure-track faculty and to be able to use it as a recruiting incentive.
  • Norm Borin will revise the proposal, submit to area chairs who will ask for input from the faculty.

5. Classroom Update

  • Terri stated that Facilities measured Room 204 and when it is divided, it appears that one new classroom will have a seating capacity 32 and the classroom may be slightly larger.
  • There is a possibility of working with the existing tiered seating with the orientation being the same.
  • This remodel will occur this summer.

6. FAR Rewards

  • Terri requested clarification on the guidelines on the use of FAR dollars allocated for 2001-02. ACs stated that the dollars could be used to support the faculty member's professional development

7. Tenure-Track Recruitment

  • Area chairs stated their needs for tenure-track faculty members-Economics is requesting two positions, Finance one, Industrial Technology is requesting three, Management three positions, Marketing one position, and Accounting will be forthcoming with its request..
  • Recruitment ads are to be written broad enough so any adjustments in the number and specialty of faculty hired can be made after the strategic plan is finalized.
  • Level of hiring and incentives to attract faculty were discussed. Further clarification on recruiting will be available after input is received from the Student Fee Initiative Committee.

8. Advisory Council

  • Members of the OCOB Advisory Council will be participating in classes on Thursday, May 16, 2002. Any additional faculty wanting to have a member participate in their class should contact Terri or area chair as soon as possible.
