Area Chairs Council
Meeting Notes - May 21, 2002

Present: Fred Abitia, Norm Borin, Doug Cerf, Barry Floyd, Tad Miller, Ken Riener, Jim Sena, Alden Shiers, Terri Swartz

1. Approval of Notes:

  • Meeting notes of the May 14, 2002, meeting were approved by email and posted on OCOB.

2. Proposal for Zero Teaching Quarter

  • Proposal incorporated all requests from previous ACC meeting.
  • Some areas did not have feedback from faculty. Area chairs were requested to seek faculty input and discuss at next ACC meeting.

3. Course Evaluation Proposal

  • Standardized set of instructions was submitted with clear instructions that faculty are not to be present when evaluations are administered. These instructions also have clear directions to student monitors where the evaluations are to be delivered.

4. Area Follow-up on Community Building

  • Areas reported little progress on community building that was developed at the April 12 college retreat

5. Student Fee Task Force

  • Student academic fee task force presented their recommendations to Terri on May 21. The group is finalizing its recommendations which will be submitted May 28.

6. Budget

  • $23.6 billion expected shortfall for the State ($75B revenue)
  • $11.5 billion in reductions in spending is expected
  • $38 million budget reduction for CSU
  • Expect a mid-year budget cut
  • The college will plan for a 5% budget reduction.

7. Advising Center

  • Kris McKinlay, the new advisor that started May 1 is getting oriented with policies and procedures in the advising center and at applicable offices on campus.
  • The timing of the degree works implementation at the college is uncertain.
  • Discussion of issues related to students attempting to return under an old (more than 10 years) catalog

8. Senior project and independent study policy was discussed

9. Advisory council meeting feedback

  • Patricia McQuaid and Kathy Lancaster's presentations on their faculty fellow programs were well received by the advisory council.
  • Advisory council members would like to be more involved with faculty and classes
  • Advisory council members would like a mixture of dinners (some more social).
  • Academic relevancy committee members felt their meeting with the UPC was productive.
