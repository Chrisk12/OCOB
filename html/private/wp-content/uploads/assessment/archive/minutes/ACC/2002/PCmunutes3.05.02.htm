Policy Council
Minutes - March 5, 2002

Present: Fred Abitia, Norm Borin, Doug Cerf, Barry Floyd, Michael Geringer, Ken Riener, Tad Miller, Jim Sena, Alden Shiers, Terri Swartz

I. Minutes

  • February 28, 2002 minutes not presented.

II. Announcements

  • The University Registration and Scheduling Committee has an opening. Terri is asking faculty for volunteer candidates.
  • There are two Senate related openings, one is for Senator and the other is on the Accreditation Senate Curriculum Committee.
  • The Policy Council's next meeting will be March 19, noon-2pm.
  • A memo from Paul Zingg asks OCOB Areas for a description of their RPT procedures. Terri will look into this matter.
  • Terri asked that ACs bring handouts whenever they present information to the Policy Council. The intention is to facilitate the understanding of material being presented.

III. Faculty Staffing Needs for AY 2002-03

  • OCOB Tenure/Tenure Track History was handed out and Terri asked for corrections.
  • Some expressed concern about Accreditation and the number of tenure/tenure track faculty on staff.
  • Reviewed and discussed each Area's faculty staffing needs for AY 2002-03. Terri requested ACs to submit their faculty staffing needs in writing.
  • After some discussion and input the decision was made to go forward with the MIS recruitment of a tenure track hire. The International Management recruitment is cancelled.

IV. New Faculty Contract

  • The new tentative union agreement for faculty and its impact on lecturers was discussed.

V. Accreditation

  • Ernst & Young Foundation has agreed to provide support for the Strategic Planning Process, but no contract has been signed yet. Terri is working with the task force. More details to follow.
  • Utilizing the Ernst & Young system does not guarantee success.
  • Other schools have been contacted and more will be contacted, especially those who have successfully been through the process. Wisconsin has given no response. San Jose State and Northridge are comparable schools and should be contacted because they have successfully been through the process.
