Undergraduate Program Committee
April 23, 2002

Minutes

The UPC met from 9:00 - 10:30. Members attending the meeting were: Robison, Borin, Crother, Peach, Shiers, Weatherford, and Cerf.

1. The committee approved the deletion of the CSC prerequisite for BUS 391 for the 2003-2005 catalog.

2. The committee considered the question of whether or not to remove BUS 101 from the catalog. The consensus was to leave the course in the catalog.

3. The committee discussed whether the UPC policy that requires a minimum grade of C- in all major and support courses should be included in the catalog. The provision was enacted sometime prior to fall 1999 but has not been generally enforced. Weatherford moved to remove the language from the UPC guidelines and Shiers seconded. Discussion ensued centering on the lack of enforcement, the necessity to have a minimum level of expertise in all business courses, and the possibility that new software will make enforcement feasible. The motion failed with a 3 - 3 vote.

Robison will request faculty input in an effort to ascertain whether or not the faculty as a whole shares these concerns, and what if any additional action should be taken.

4. The matter of crosslisting courses was revisited. After discussion, Borin moved to leave the present UPC policy unchanged. That is: " Courses are to be 'housed' in their functional area, but may be taught by any qualified faculty." Peach seconded. Motion passed, 3 - 2 with one abstention.

5. The committee addressed the list of Skills and Competencies. No changes were proposed. The committee will meet with members of the OCOB Advisory Board on May 16 to further discuss this topic.


6. Shiers will distribute hard copies of the Economics area review which will be the primary topic at the next meeting

The meeting was adjourned. Next meeting to be May 7th.
