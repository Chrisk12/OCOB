BUS 401 Seminar in General Management and Strategy  
1.  To introduce the concept of strategic management, identifying concepts and tools which will enhance students ability to understand and formulate effective strategies.
2.  To develop capabilities in identifying and analyzing key environmental and organizational variables that may influence an organizations performance.
3.  To increase students awareness of the complexity of the general managers position, including the challenges of managing for the benefit of the overall organization rather than for any particular functional area.
4.  To enhance students skills in analyzing complex strategic issues, as well as in developing practical plans for effectively implementing recommendations for addressing these issues.
5.  To encourage students to think actively about problem situations and the variety of options to deal with them. This pertains not only to strategic business problems the course will discuss but also to how each student will strategically approach successfully completing the course.
6.  To get students to think outside the normal paradigms of academic learning and study. To have you step back and ask big picture questions about why things happen and what the possible choices are. To allow you to build off of your peers experiences and insights.
