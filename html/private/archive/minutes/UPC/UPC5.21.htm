Undergraduate Program Committee
May 21, 2002

Minutes

Present: Robison (chair), Borin, Cerf, Crother, Peach, Shiers, Wheatherford

1. The x-class proposal for a senior project class, BUS 464, was discussed. The committee decided to return the proposal to management for revisions to Sections A and B. The committee wanted a more detailed description of the course content, and a course outline for the simulation option. The revised proposal will be sent to committee members for approval.

2. The Economics program review was approved unanimously.

3. The previous meeting with the Advisory Board Academic Relevancy Committee was discussed. Next year the UPC will meet with the Relevancy Committee twice, once in the fall and once in the spring. This will allow for earlier involvement of the Relevancy Committee.

The committee discussed the issue of setting minimum standards for aggregate coverage of skills and competencies. No action on this item was taken because another group is working on this issue for accreditation, and the current skills and competencies may be augmented.

Work ethics of students was discussed. The Relevancy Committee had pointed out that it is important for students to know that showing up at work on time and meeting deadlines is essential. The committee discussed the culture of the classroom and codes of conduct. The committee felt that the appropriate strategic planning group should look at this issue.

4. The current OCOB policy requiring a grade of C- or better in all major and support courses for all OCOB programs was discussed. The UPC had previously invited faculty input on this issue. Jack informed the committee that responses from the faculty were 10 against the current policy and 7 in favor of the current policy. The discussion noted that the Economics and IT areas opposed the policy and BUS had trouble enforcing the policy. It was pointed out that when Degree Works comes online it can be used for enforcement, but the date when Degree Works will become available is unknown. A motion to eliminate the OCOB policy that requires a grade of C- or better in all major and support courses for all OCOB programs was made and seconded. The motion passed: 5 yes, 1 abstain.

Discussion of enforcing the C- policy for BUS 401 and BUS 342 followed. The committee concluded that any area could propose the C- policy for their courses. Enforcing the policy will the responsibility of the area.

Meeting adjourned.

Next meeting: next fall. Volunteers for next year's Committee Chair will be gladly accepted.
