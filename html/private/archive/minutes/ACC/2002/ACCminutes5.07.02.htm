Area Chairs Council
Meeting Notes - May 7 , 2002

Present: Fred Abitia, Norm Borin, Barry Floyd, Tad Miller, Ken Riener, Jim Sena, Alden Shiers, Terri Swartz

1. Approval of Notes:

  • Meeting notes of the April 23, 2002, were approved by email and posted via email.

2. Course Evaluation Proposal

  • A revision of the "Guidelines for Administration of Student Course Evaluations" was submitted. This proposal will be submitted to faculty by their area chairs for review and discussed at the next ACC meeting.

3. Senior Project and Independent Study Course Proposal

  • This proposal had one change and area chairs will review with their faculty for their input. The proposal will be discussed at the next ACC meeting.

4. Technology Rooms

  • The university has requested input from colleges regarding technical needs that are necessary to create "smart" classrooms. Terri requested area chairs to discuss with their faculty what technical needs they might have for various classrooms.

5. Budget

  • The University received a budget de-allocation for 2001-02 of $1.5 million. The campus is able to cover approximately half the cost with the balance being passed down to the various colleges and departments. The OCOB's share is $33,387.
  • The April 2% GSI, the July 2% GSI and SSIs plus the 7% department chairs/heads increases have all been funded for 2002-2003 year.
  • It is anticipated that the University will receive a budget reduction of approximately $3 million for the 2002-03 year but final approval of this budget will be known on May 14 when the governor's final budget review is published. If the CSU reduces Cal Poly's budget by $3 million, then the OCOB's reduction in budget will be approximately $214,000.
  • The MOU that was approved included a 3 ½ % salary increase for faculty in 2002-03 but as of this date there is no funding for this increase. There is no contract provision stating this increase is dependent upon funding.

6. Administrative Time for Area Chairs

  • Currently area chairs administrative time base is .27 (4 units). Effective Spring
    Quarter 2002, the time base for area chairs will be increased to .50 (7 ½ units). Chairs will still teach one class a quarter.

7. FAR Rewards - Student Assistants

  • Student assistant allocations from the FAR rewards process will be allocated in dollars to each area because faculty hire students at different rates of pay. The allocation will be $8 per hour.
  • Last year no faculty was granted student assistant allocation for professional development and that will be the procedure for this year's allocation.

8. Zero Teaching Assignments

  • The Council discussed the issue of granting untenured faculty one quarter of a zero teaching assignment.
  • Ken Riener, Barry Floyd and Norm Borin will draft a proposal to present to the ACC.

9. Skill Enhancement Support

  • Areas were requested to submit the dollars needed to cover costs for training courses for faculty to update skills that are necessary to teach new technology, etc. in the curriculum to Terri by May 17.

10. Faculty Mentoring

  • Area chairs were asked to consider a faculty mentoring program in the college. It will be discussed at a future meeting of the ACC.

11. Community Building - April 5, 2002, College-wide Retreat

  • Chairs are to email Terri an update on the community building status for their area.
