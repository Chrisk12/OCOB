Dr. Roche enjoys an international reputation for research in Finance related to asset pricing, investment under uncertainty and portfolio choices. He has been involved in projects exploring the impact of margin requirements on portfolio strategies in an attempt to explain why many investors choose to hold under-diversified portfolios despite conventional wisdom. He has also studied the effects of drawdown constraints on wealth management and more recently, the role of the difference of opinion among investors in the formation of speculative bubbles.

Hervé has been a reviewer for numerous academic journals such as Financial Management, the Journal of Economic Theory, the Review of Financial Studies as well as for the prestigious National Science Foundation. His work has been published in the Journal of Economic Theory and in the Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control and has been presented at more than 35 conferences and workshops worldwide, including recently at the annual meetings of the Econometric Society and the American Finance Association and, at the Brongniart Palace, formerly the home of the Paris stock exchange.

Hervé received his PhD in Economics from the University of Pennsylvania He earned his MS in Civil engineering from the Ecole Nationale des Ponts et Chaussées in Paris, France. He spent 7 years as an Assistant Professor at the Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM), Mexico top business school and one of the top three leading business schools in Latin America. He has been an Associate Professor in Finance at CalPoly since 2007 where he teaches a class on portfolio management and security analysis and an advanced course on derivative products.
