Dr. Mintz enjoys an international reputation for research and teaching ethics in business and accounting. He has published two textbooks on the topic. His most recent publication is Ethical Obligations and Decision Making in Accounting: Text and Cases. Dr. Mintz has published dozens of research papers in the areas of business ethics, accounting ethics, corporate governance and international accounting. Dr. Mintz teaches a course on accounting ethics and serves on university committees that evaluate curriculum coverage and assessment of learning in ethics.

Dr. Mintz has developed numerous ethics training programs for organizations including JC Penney, EDP Systems, The State Auditorsâ€™ Office in Austin, Texas, and the Department of Health in Texas. He also develops and teaches continuing education courses in ethics for CPAs. His courses are used in sixteen states to meet the continuing education requirement for re-licensing.

Dr. Mintz is a widely sought out speaker at ethics and academic conferences. He has presented at : The Board of Director and Corporate Governance Research Conference in Henley, England; Global Finance & Research Conference in London; The Institute of Chartered Accountants in Trinidad & Tobago; Association of Asian-Pacific Accountants in Bangkok, Thailand; and the Asian International Business Association in Shanghai, China.

Dr. Mintz has received several awards for his professional and academic accomplishments including the Outstanding Speaker Award given by the Global Finance & Research Conference; the Professional Service award given by the Texas Society of CPAs; the Academic Service Award of the Inland Empire Hispanic Chamber of Commerce; the Outstanding Leadership Award given by San Francisco State University; and the Faculty Excellence Award given by the California Society of CPAs. Steven holds a Ph.D in International Accounting from the George Washington University and is a Certified Public Accountant in New York.
