Adam Stephens’s background includes a great variety of different skills and applications including metals fabrication, product development, composites fabrication, robotics, process selection and design as well as packaging research and testing. Stephens has launched two companies: Berserker Cycle Design, which specializes in full suspension recumbent tricycles: Phoenix Precision Machining and Prototyping, is just getting started and primarily deals with product design and prototyping. In terms of research, Stephens works with Dr. Jay Singh primarily in areas including Cold Chain packaging, the reuse of corrugated paper board and material and package testing. Recent academic and professional honors include: Pacific Coast Business Times, 40 Under 40 class of 2009, Outstanding Graduate Student Nominee 2007, Class of 2007 Industrial Technology Master’s Program Valedictorian.

Stephens can often be found working with students and other entrepreneurs in developing new products and technologies. Stephens worked with one student, as technical advisor, for a design project for in a renewable Electronics Packaging system. The design took fourth place overall in the Innovation Quest Competition and the student is investigating the commercialization of the design. He has also worked with such companies as Cospheric Microspheres, Sunrise Manufacturing, Westpak, TAD Gear and Delta Gear, Inc.

Adam received his BS and MS in industrial Technology from Cal Poly State University. He started as a Graduate teaching associate and continued on as the acting Lab Coordinator as well as a part time lecturer after graduation. In his time since graduation he has played a significant part with the Industrial Technology Robotics Club which competes nationally every year and is also acting as a Staff advisor for Industrial Technologies Poly Pack Packaging club. Stephens’s next step is to enroll and complete a PHD in Packaging Design and Mechatronics.
