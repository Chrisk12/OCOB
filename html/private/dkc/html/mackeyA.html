Dr. Mackey’s research is related to executive labor markets, executive compensation, and corporate social responsibility. Amongst her several academic and professional honors are: Wiley Blackwell Outstanding Dissertation award from the Business Policy and Strategy Division of the Academy of Management, Editorial Board member of the Journal of Management, numerous research grants and fellowships, and Advisory Board Member for Management CV.

Alison is very active in the academic community. She currently serves within the Academy of Management on the Business Policy and Strategy Division’s Research Committee. She frequently serves as a referee at the top peer-reviewed management journals. Within the Strategic Management Society she is working with the launch committee for the new Human Capital and Competitive Advantage Research Interest Group and as a conference reviewer for the annual international Strategic Management Society Conference. She has published in peer reviewed journals such Academy of Management Review, Strategic Management Journal, and Business & Society. As a consultant she draws on her background in organizational behavior, working in the outplacement industry and in organizational development within the health care industry.

Alison received her PhD in Business Policy and Strategy from Ohio State University. She earned her MA in Organizational Behavior and her BA in Economics from Brigham Young University. Prior to her appointment on the faculty at Cal Poly, she was on the faculty at Texas A&M University’s Mays Business School teaching in both the undergraduate and graduate programs there. Currently she teaches business strategy in the undergraduate program at Cal Poly.
