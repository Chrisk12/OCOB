Chris Carr has served as a faculty member at the Cal Poly Orfalea College of Business since 1998.  He has received the College’s Student Selection for Outstanding Faculty Member Award on two occasions.  He has published in a variety of business journals and legal journals.  Shortly after promotion to full professor he was appointed to serve as the Orfalea College’s Associate Dean of Graduate Programs and Faculty Development.  Professor Carr served in this leadership position from Fall 2004 to Fall 2009.  During this five year time period he was an integral part of the leadership team that strengthened the college’s graduate business programs, helped lead the college in its successful AACSB re-accreditation efforts, and for the first time helped the college achieve a ranking in Business Week’s top 100 undergraduate business programs.  Cal Poly and UC Berkeley were the only public universities in California to make that list.

Professor Carr is passionate about global business and education.  He designed, successfully lobbied for university approval, executes and leads the Orfalea College’s graduate level international business field course to the People’s Republic of China and recently India.  He consults with firms seeking to enter the Chinese market.  Further, in 2008 he was selected to serve as a Fulbright Senior Specialist to consult with foreign universities and business schools in Asia.    

Professor Carr enjoys teaching, research, service and consulting – and views these components as synergistic.  Outside Cal Poly, his interests include travel, anything to do with Greater China, camping, hiking, reading, sports, museums, foreign films, music and wine tasting.
