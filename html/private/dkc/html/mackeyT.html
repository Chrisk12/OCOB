Dr. Mackey’s academic research covers areas such as the relationship between corporate diversification and firm value, Bayesian modeling, and corporate social responsibility. Amongst his several academic and professional honors are: Honorable Mention for Strategic Management Society’s Best Conference Paper Prize, Booz Allen Hamilton/ Strategic Management Society PhD Fellowship, and Editorial Board member for the Journal of Management.

Tyson’s research has been featured in the Academy of Management Review and Academy of Management Best Paper Proceedings. He is an active member of the Academy of Management and Strategic Management Society. He has presented his research at over 15 conferences and universities as an invited speaker. Tyson frequently serves as an ad hoc referee at top management journals in addition to his duties as an editorial board member for the Journal of Management.

Tyson received his PhD in Business Policy and Strategy from Ohio State University. He earned his MBS and BS in Economics from Brigham Young University. Previously he taught in the undergraduate, honors, and graduate programs at Texas A&M University. Currently he teaches business policy and strategy to undergraduates and MBA students at Cal Poly.
