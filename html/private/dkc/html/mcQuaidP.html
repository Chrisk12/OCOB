Dr. McQuaid enjoys an international reputation in the fields of software quality and software testing. Amongst her academic and professional honors are: Winner of the “Best Paper Award” at the 2008 South East European Software Testing Conference, held in Bulgaria; winner of the “Best Presentation Award” at The Second World Congress for Software Quality, in Japan in 2000, and the winner of the “Best Research Paper Award” in the field of software metrics at The Fifth European Conference on Software Quality, in Ireland, 1996.

Dr. McQuaid is the co-founder and President of the American Software Testing Qualifications Board (ASTQB), a national organization that certifies software testing professionals. There are over 50 countries involved in this initiative.

She is an Associate Editor of the Software Quality Professional Journal, the official journal of the Software Division of the American Society for Quality. Additionally, she has been on ASQ’s Software Division Council since 1999.

She recently was awarded a “Women of Distinction, the Progress for Women Award”. The Women of Distinction Awards go to women who have made significant contributions to the quality of life throughout their communities. The Woman of Progress award goes to a woman whose commitment to a particular program or issue of special relevance to women has helped to improve the quality of life. Hers was awarded for her contributions to technology.

She has contributed numerous articles to such trade journals as ASQ’s Software Quality Professional, IEEE IT Professional, Journal of Software Process Improvement and Practice Journal, and Crosstalk, The Journal of Defense Software Engineering.

She has taught in both the colleges of Business and Engineering. Her work experience is in Information Systems auditing in both the banking and manufacturing industries.
