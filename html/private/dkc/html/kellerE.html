Dr. Keller has over forty years of commercial and academic experience. He has developed an expertise in most areas of financial accounting with special emphasis in the areas of Governmental and Not-for-Profit Entities and Securities and Exchange Commission Regulations and Filings.

He has served on the American Institute of CPAs (“AICPA”) State and Local Government Accounting Committee, the SEC Regulations Committee, and the Practice Section of the CPA Exam Committee. He has served as a Technical Consultant to the AICPA Examination Division since 1993. He has also served on a Brookings Institute Task Force to examine accounting issues regarding intangibles.
Earl has consulted on litigation cases for the SEC, and for prior officers of Enron and AIG, among others. He consulted on and participated in rate making cases for the Public Service Commission of Wisconsin and for public utilities.

He has served on numerous College and University committees at both the University of Michigan and Cal Poly. While at the University of Michigan, he Chaired six accounting dissertations, Co-chaired two accounting dissertations and one finance dissertation, and was a member of four accounting, one finance, two international business and one statistics dissertation(s). Earl was the Accounting PhD Advisor at the University of Michigan for a number of years.

Over the past forty years, Earl has taught most of the financial accounting courses that are (were) offered at Cal Poly and the University of Michigan. He was also active in offering Executive Education courses at the University of Michigan. For example, he offered the accounting and regulatory section of the UM Public Utility Executive Program for 15 years. He has developed his own teaching materials for most of his course offerings.

Earl has published extensively and has served on Editorial boards.
