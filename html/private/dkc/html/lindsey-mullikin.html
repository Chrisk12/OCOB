Dr. Mullikin enjoys a reputation for research related to pricing and consumer behavior. Her most recent research addresses the reduction in consumer price search due to pricing tactics.  Her research has been extended into the on-line environment.  Her award winning research has been published in numerous journals, such as the Journal of Academy of Marketing Sciences, the Journal of Retailing, and the Journal of Business Research.  She won the Emerald Literati Network Award for Excellence in 2007. 

She has chaired conference tracks for the American Marketing Association and regularly presents her own research at national conferences.  She served on the editorial review board of the Journal of Product and Brand Management. She is a member of the American Marketing Association and the Association of Consumer Research. 

Her MBA and PhD are from the University of Arizona.  She negotiated pricing and contract conditions of missile weapon system while employed at the McDonnell Douglas Corporation.

Dr. Mullikin’s teaching expertise is in product management, consumer behavior, and in graduate level marketing management. 

She had extensive experience teaching working professionals through her previous graduate classes on Consumer Behavior and Marketing Strategy.  She has served as faculty manager of Management Consultant Field Experiences with companies as Procter and Gamble and Allen and Gerristsen.  She has taught executives at forums such as the Women’s Leadership Center and has chaired their national conferences.
