Professor Burgunder is highly acclaimed as one of the most innovative teachers at Cal Poly, having won innumerable honors for instructional effectiveness, including the University’s prestigious Distinguished Teaching Award. He is respected nationwide as a pioneer in designing technology law courses for students in business and technological fields. Professor Burgunder is the sole author of Legal Aspects of Managing Technology, which is perhaps the leading textbook in the field and is currently in its 5th edition. He also was a founding member of the Technology Law Section of the Academy of Legal Studies in Business, and served as its Chair during the organization’s first two years of operation.

Professor Burgunder has published a wide array of articles in various prestigious journals primarily dealing with intellectual property and technology law topics, but also in other fields such as finance and marketing. He also co-authored the textbook, Business, Government, and Public Policy, with Dan Bertozzi, Jr. In addition, he has been involved with several professional organizations, and previously served as president of the Western Academy of Legal Studies in Business

Professor Burgunder received an A.B. from Dartmouth College, and earned a J.D. and an M.B.A. from Stanford University. He worked at Patton Boggs, a law firm in Washington, D.C, before coming to Cal Poly in 1983.
