Dr. Metcalf is Professor and Area Chair, Marketing at the Orfalea College of Business at California Polytechnic State University. Dr. Metcalf has twenty-five years of experience teaching high-technology marketing and e-commerce, international marketing, business-to-business marketing and sales. Currently, she specializes in revealing customer insights through qualitative research methods, as well as new product development and launch.

Over the past 10 years, she has worked with students on various product development and launch projects for approximately 100 companies.

While on a professional leave of absence from Cal Poly, Dr. Metcalf supported strategy development and articulation at Philips, as well as new product development. She incubated and nurtured relationships with research faculty and their graduate students at major research universities in the U.S. and worked with Philips businesses worldwide to establish the appropriate links back into the company.

Dr. Metcalf’s scholarly interests are in the areas of package design for marketability, innovation and regional economic development, and international business negotiation. Her research appears in the Journal of World Business, International Journal of Cross-Cultural Management, European Journal of Marketing, Industrial Marketing Management, and others. She also has presented research at multiple academic conferences including the Academy of International Business.

Dr. Metcalf is advisor to the Cal Poly Chapter of the Wheelchair Foundation, and has led student groups on humanitarian missions to Guatemala, Belize, Argentina, Peru, and Mexico.  In its 9th year, the Cal Poly Chapter of the Wheelchair Foundation has raised almost ½ million dollars to pay for wheelchairs for people in other countries who need them but cannot afford them.
