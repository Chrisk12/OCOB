Dr. Arline Savage received her doctoral degree in Accounting from the University of Port Elizabeth in South Africa. She is also a Chartered Accountant. She has worked in industry, the accounting profession, and as a consultant. As a professor, she has taught a wide variety of courses at universities in South Africa, Canada and the United States.

Arline is internationally recognized in her discipline. Her areas of expertise are Financial Reporting, Financial Analysis and Valuation, Fraud Examination, Business Process Analysis, Internal Control Assessment, and Information Systems. Among her academic honors in the United States are six best conference paper awards and two keynote speaker invitations. She is recipient of the Deloitte Faculty Fellowship Award, the Fluor Corporation Excellence in Teaching Award, and the Ernst & Young Outstanding Educator Award. Arline is past President of the Accounting Information Systems (AIS) Educator Association, which provides hands-on computer training and mentorship for accounting faculty across the United States, and is founding editor-in-chief of the associationâ€™s AIS Educator Journal. She also serves on the editorial board of the International Journal of Business Information Systems.

She has 32 academic and professional publications, including two Elsevier research monographs and an advanced financial accounting textbook. Some of her articles appear in the Journal of Information Systems, The CPA Journal, Accounting and the Public Interest, Issues in Accounting Education, and Advances in Environmental Accounting and Management.

In the United States, Arline consults for private equity investors on various accounting and finance projects in the food wholesale, apparel, engineering, and manufacturing industries, and has served as financial advisor to the chairman of an audit committee of a public company. She recently served as interim CFO for an engineering company during the post-acquisition phase and performs business valuations for acquisition and accounting purposes.
