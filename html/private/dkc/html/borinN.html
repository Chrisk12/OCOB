Dr. Borin is full professor in the marketing area at the Orfalea College of Business. He has taught courses in the areas of marketing strategy, promotion, product management, promotions strategy and quantitative business analysis. He has been a visiting professor at the Seinajoki Polytechnic Business School in Seinajoki Finland and has taught at the Mcintire School of Business at the University of Virginia.

Dr. Borin has published in the areas of retail shelf space management optimization, retail/supplier channel power, internet advertising allocation and marketing and business curriculum development and pedagogy. His current work is in the area of sustainable marketing practices in which he has conducted a stream of research investigating consumer reaction to green marketing labels, small retail sustainable marketing strategies and an analysis of the consumer impact of different sustainable business strategies.

Dr. Borin’s work has been published in the Journal of Retailing, Decision Sciences, Journal of Internet Advertising, Journal of Marketing Education, Journal of Education for Business. His article “Market Power and Performance: A Cross-Industry Analysis of Manufacturers and Retailers” received the best article of the year from the Journal of Retailing. As part of an international venture he is author of two chapters in Berkshire Publishing’s forthcoming Encyclopedia of Sustainability.

Dr. Borin has won numerous teaching awards including the Orfalea College Business’ Distinguished Teacher of the Year and the Marketing Area’s Outstanding Professor.

Professor Borin lives in San Luis Obispo with his wife Angela and dog Shasta.
