Dr. Andreas Simon is an assistant professor of accounting at the Orfalea College of Business at Cal Poly. His research interests include accounting-based equity valuation, earnings forecasting and capital markets research. He is currently investigating how capital market intermediaries process financial statement data and make investment decisions, as well as the role of accounting information in security analysis. As an early career academic he has already had success with publishing in top-ranking journals. For example, his paper “The Effect of a Change in Analyst Composition on Analyst Forecast Accuracy: Evidence from US Cross Listing”, published in The Journal of International Accounting Research, was listed on Social Science Research Network’s (SSRN) Top Ten download list for Corporate Governance, Disclosure, Internal Control and Risk Management. A paper in the same research stream “Would you Bet Your Savings on Today’s Best Analysts?” was recently listed on SSRN’s Top Ten download list for Capital Market and Market Efficiency Research. He was awarded the Cal Poly State Faculty Support Grant in 2009 for continued academic research.

Andreas has presented his work at various national and international conferences in Accounting and Finance including the American Accounting Association Meetings, Financial Accounting Research Section Meetings, Western Region American Accounting Association Meetings, Australian and New Zealand Accounting and Finance Association meetings, and Asian Finance Association Meetings. He also conducted seminars at The University of Utah, The University of Michigan, The University of New South Wales (Australia), ESADE Business School (Spain), and Bocconi University (Italy). At Cal Poly, he organizes the accounting research seminar at the Orfalea College of Business.

Dr. Andreas Simon earned his Ph.D. in Accounting from the University of Queensland in Brisbane, Australia in 2008. He also has a Master’s degree from the University of Applied Sciences in Berlin, Germany, with emphasis in Accounting and Finance. Prior to joining Cal Poly he taught at top-ranking MBA programs at the Ross School of Business, University of Michigan and ESADE Business School in Barcelona, Spain. He has been a weekly visiting professor at the Anderson School of Management, UCLA since Winter 2008. Prior to graduate school, Andreas worked as financial analyst for Landesbank Holding AG in Berlin, Germany and as associate in assurance and business advisory services – financial services at PriceWaterhouseCoopers in Berlin, Germany. He is a certified financial analyst in Germany. Andreas is a member of the American Accounting Association and the Australian and New Zealand Accounting and Finance Association.
