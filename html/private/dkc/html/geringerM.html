Dr. Geringer is an award winning researcher, teacher, and consultant, with an international reputation for his work in strategic management, international business, joint ventures and alliances, mergers and acquisitions, entrepreneurship, venture capital, and the management of technologic and human resources. Amongst his academic and professional honors are: University Distinguished Teacher Award and International Educator Awards from Cal Poly; Saastamoinen Foundation Chair from Helsinki University of Economics; Outstanding Professor Award from the Korean Executive MBA Program and Poznan School of Banking; Decade Award from Journal of International Business Studies; George & Sara McCune Best Paper Award from Group and Organization Management; and Division Chair for the Academy of Management and Administrative Sciences Association of Canada.

He has nearly 20 years of consulting experience with private and public sector organizations on 5 continents, including Nokia, Lucent, Perlos, Eastman Chemical Company, Okobank, Northern Telecom, Rautaruukki, Sonera, Y.I.T., Jiangsu Telecom Industrial Corporation, Investment Canada, UPM-Kymmene, Hansabank, Economic Council of Canada, and Industry, Science and Technology Canada.

He has authored or edited 16 books and monographs (in English, French, Chinese and Indonesian) and published over 115 papers in leading journals, including Journal of International Business Studies, Academy of Management Journal, Strategic Management Journal, and Journal of Applied Psychology. He serves on Editorial Boards of 6 scholarly journals and has received 11 “best paper” awards from scholarly organizations. He has taught at universities in the U.S., Canada, Finland, Poland, Hungary, United Kingdom, Australia, Singapore, Taiwan, China, Korea, Indonesia and South Africa, receiving over 20 “best professor” awards in recognition of his instructional performance. He has extensive leadership experience in industry, university, and not for profit organizations, including Licensing Executives Society, Academy of Management, Administrative Sciences Association of Canada, and A.I.E.S.E.C.
