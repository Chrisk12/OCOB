Dr. Griggs is a Professor of Information Systems in the Management Area. He teaches a
wide range of courses in the areas of Systems Analysis and Design, Electronic
Commerce, Web Development, Multimedia Systems, and Strategic Management.

His areas of research interest include e-Government, knowledge management, human
interface design, Accounting Information Systems, ERP Systems, emerging
technologies, and forecasting. His work has appeared in a wide variety of journals
including the Communications of the ACM, the International Journal of Forecasting, the
Journal of Electronic Government, the Journal of Organizational Behavior and Human
Decision Processes, and many others. He has presented his research at a broad variety
of national and international academic conferences.

Dr. Griggs has consulted in the areas of business process re-engineering, knowledge
management, and knowledge-based system design and development. He has worked
on a variety of defense and government related projects with DARPA, the U.S. Navy,
and CINCPACFLT (Pearl Harbor) and many others. He has held academic appointments
including the University of Hawaii, the University of Arizona, McGill University, and
Concordia University.
