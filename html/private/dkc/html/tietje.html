Brian Tietje (pronounced “tee-gee”) is a Professor of Marketing and serves as the Associate Dean and Interim Director of Graduate Programs at the Orfalea College of Business. His undergraduate business degree is from Bowling Green State University (1998), his MBA is from the University of Hawaii (1994), and his Ph.D. in marketing is from the University of Washington (1999).

Dr. Tietje is passionate about student learning. He has won several teaching awards during his career at Cal Poly, is the co-author of an introduction to business textbook titled “Anybody’s Business” (with Barbara Van Syckle) published by Prentice Hall, and he has authored the Wall Street Journal weekly educator’s review since 1999. As Associate Dean, he spearheads efforts to improve student success and graduation rates and is leading the effort to develop alternative modes of course delivery to improve student learning and resource efficiencies. He is also the principal facilitator of the College’s assessment of learning program.

Dr. Tietje has published academic articles on the topics of marketing research, marketing strategy, branding, industrial sales, and consumer psychology. The current focus of his research is on the application of lean principles to the sales process to increase value creation, particularly in industrial and technical sales.

Dr. Tietje also has extensive business experience. He teaches executive education courses, serves on the board of directors of Weatherby, Inc., has engaged in several consulting projects, and has corporate sales experience with firms including Culligan, Northwestern Mutual, Unilever, and Johnson & Johnson Consumer Products, Inc.
