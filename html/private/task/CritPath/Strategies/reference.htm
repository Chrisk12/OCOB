Positioning Statement

 

The task force has concluded that a key distinguishing feature of the OCOB is that it prepares students to hit the ground running while also providing them with the sophistication to become leaders of organizations.  The task force believes that a primary role of concentrations is to provide sufficient depth in a course of study to enable OCOB students to provide immediate value to business organizations.  This is not to say that concentrations should be viewed as more important than other disciplines, for a successful businessperson must have a breadth of understanding in numerous important areas.  Rather, concentrations are merely a logical way to organize academic programs to make the college distinctive.

To market the MBA program, a goal is to build on Cal Poly values, including, but not limited to the following:

 

     •   Learn by doing

     •   Small class size

     •   Excellent, engaged faculty

     •   Teamwork

     •   Close interaction with industry

     •   Emphasis on career objectives with a focus on job placement

 

Students admitted into the MBA program must meet minimum quality requirements. The current minimum quantitative requirements are as follows: GPA 3.00 during last 90 units of Undergraduate program and a score of 530 on the GMAT exam with an emphasis on the quantitative portion of the exam. The long term goal is to continue to recruit and admit high quality students and to work in as much as possible to raise these standards. Current average GPA and GMAT scores are 3.2 and 595 respectively.
