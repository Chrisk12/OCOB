The Reporting Expectation for each of the items requested below is a written report with total length not to exceed 20 pages (selected appendices may be added if absolutely necessary).  This report will be due to AACSB on or before January 15, 2004.

 

A.  Standard M. 1:  The school must have a clear and published mission statement that is subjected to periodic review and revised as needed.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  Describe the process established to assure periodic review of the mission and values statement and for annual renewal of the strategic plan.

 

B.  Standard M.3:  The school must specify the educational objectives of each degree program offered and identify the characteristics of students and other constituents served by each of those degree programs.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  List and describe the broad educational objectives of each degree and show how these are aligned with the new mission statement.

 

C. Standard M.4:  The school must specify its relative emphasis on teaching, intellectual contributions, and service.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  The team found evidence of some disagreement and/or miscommunication among the faculty and between the dean’s office and the faculty concerning the relative emphasis on teaching, intellectual contributions and service that is desired for successful achievement of tenure and promotion. While guidelines exist in official policies and documents, the application and interpretation of these guidelines, particularly those concerning teaching and research, appear to be unclear among groups of faculty and across different departments/areas.  Provide a self-diagnosis of this problem and the ways in which it has been addressed.

 

          D. Standard M.5:  The school’s activities must be consistent with its mission.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  The new mission statement includes shared values.  The team did find evidence of shared understanding about some of these values, particularly of “learn-by-doing.”  In a few instances, most notably “openness and cooperation” and “mutual respect and collegiality,” there appears to be a need for further growth and reinforcement of these values within the College.  Introduce selected regular activities designed to encourage the shared values and describe these in the report. Make clear how further development of the desired shared values will be addressed.

 

E.  Standard FD.2.c:  The school should demonstrate continuous efforts to achieve demographic diversity in its faculty.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  Within the guidance of California’s laws and educational policies, identify and describe ways to ensure continuous efforts to achieve demographic diversity.

 

F.  Standard FD.3.a:  Processes should be in place to determine appropriate teaching assignments and service workloads, to guide and mentor faculty, and to provide adequate support for activities that implement the school’s mission.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  Report on the approved version of the plan for mentoring faculty, providing the implementation plan and its early results.

 

G.  Standard FD.3.b:  A formal, periodic review process should exist for reappointment, promotion, and tenure decisions that produces results consistent with the school’s mission and objectives.  Within the total criteria used for evaluation, attention should be given to course development, effective teaching, and instructional innovations.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  The OCOB has detailed written procedures addressing this standard.  In addition, one of the strategic initiatives targeted for this academic year is to clarify the tenure and promotion process by a review and realignment of the College’s reporting procedures and documents (RPT and FAR).  Describe the achievement of this initiative including faculty approval and implementation procedures for the new documents. 

 

Also, the team found evidence of variability year-to-year and across areas in the way the 109 forms for promotion and tenure are completed.  Address the apparent inconsistency by providing uniform guidelines for the College in the way that data should be included and how it should be presented (possible examples include: specify a standard form for  citation of journal articles and other scholarly works; provide examples of ways to describe the type and level of scholarly journal; provide guidance on the types of publications and other forms of scholarly activity that will be valued by the college in light of its new mission, perhaps citing Boyer’s classification or the classification proposed in the draft new standards for AACSB.)

 

H.  Standard C.1.2.d: The school should state additional requirements for completion of the undergraduate business degree consistent with its mission.  Majors or specializations should be consistent with the institutional mission and the availability of resources.

 

Accomplishment Expectation: One of the strategic initiatives for the current year is the management of undergraduate degree concentrations. Describe the process created and implemented to reach the strategic objective of managing undergraduate concentrations, including guidelines for the addition and removal of concentrations.  

 

          I.  Standard C.1.3.a through e (all having to do with the MBA curriculum):

 

Accomplishment Expectation: Revision of the MBA degree is one of the five strategic objectives for the current year.   Report on the process and end result of the revision of the MBA degree including the process that will be used for implementing the revision.

 

J.  Standard C.2.2:  Each degree program should be systematically monitored to assess its effectiveness and should be revised to reflect new objectives and to incorporate improvements based on contemporary theory and practice.

 

Accomplishment Expectation:  Describe how the responsible individuals and groups will satisfy this standard with regard to the undergraduate and graduate degrees in light of the new mission and strategic plan. Also provide a process for realigning the deployment of faculty and administrative resources to sustain the undergraduate and graduate programs with continuous improvement.
